Overview

Robots should understand brief speech grounded in gaze and gestures.

EDITH captures the human's first-person view, gaze, and speech with smart glasses, then uses those signals alongside robot observations to infer intent. The key idea is to separate intent inference from low-level control: a VLM produces grounded subtasks and a VLA policy executes them.

Method

Hardware streams feed a hierarchical policy.

The method follows the paper's Section 3: EDITH first captures human signals through smart glasses, then runs a high-level policy and low-level policy asynchronously through a shared task queue.

Hardware System

Capturing human signals via smart glasses.

EDITH streams the human's first-person view, gaze, and speech in real time, then synchronizes them with robot observations.

  • Uses Project Aria glasses to capture first-person RGB, gaze, and speech.
  • Transcribes speech into language instructions ℓt.
  • Aligns human signals with robot observations ot by timestamp.

Evaluation Tasks

Three tasks require grounding underspecified language in nonverbal signals.

In each task, the language instruction is intentionally incomplete: the robot must interpret gaze or pointing gestures to identify the target objects and complete the requested subtasks.

Task 01

Muffin-Serving

Six muffins are densely arranged. The human requests three with a single instruction while pointing at each target.

3 subtasks 80 demos

Task 02

Tumbler-Sorting

Five tumblers and two baskets are placed on the table. The human points to tumblers and baskets to specify a multi-step sorting request.

4 subtasks 80 demos

Task 03

Tool-Passing

While assembling a metal stand, the human briefly asks for a driver and a metal profile; gaze identifies the intended tools.

2 subtasks 120 demos

Results

EDITH improves task success while reducing the user's instruction burden.

Results are reported with success rate (SR) and task progress (TP) over 48 trials per task per method, plus a user study on instruction workload.

Average SR

59.7%

EDITH average success rate across Muffin-Serving, Tumbler-Sorting, and Tool-Passing.

Average TP

84.7%

Average fraction of subtasks completed per trial by EDITH.

Ablation

Keyframes matter.

Removing Ckey causes a 49.9% average drop in success rate and 51.5% drop in task progress.

Robustness

Distraction remains manageable.

Under phone-checking distraction, EDITH shows only a 0.5% relative drop in task progress.

Interpretation

Hierarchy handles brief intent.

The high-level policy monitors human signals separately while the low-level policy continues execution.
